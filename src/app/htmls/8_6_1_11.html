842 Leases

30 - Lessor

> Sales-Type and Direct Financing Leases

842-30-40-1 At the commencement date, a lessor shall derecognize the carrying amount of the underlying asset (if previously recognized) unless the lease is a sales-type lease and collectibility of the lease payments is not probable (see paragraph 842-30-25-3)

>> Lease Termination

842-30-40-2 If a sales-type lease or a direct financing lease is terminated before the end of the lease term, a lessor shall do all of the following:

a. Test the net investment in the lease for impairment in accordance with Topic 310 on receivables and recognize any impairment loss identified

b. Reclassify the net investment in the lease to the appropriate category of asset in accordance with other Topics, measured at the sum of the carrying amounts of the lease receivable (less any amounts still expected to be received by the lessor) and the residual asset

c. Account for the underlying asset that was the subject of the lease in accordance with other Topics.

>> Subleases

842-30-40-3 If the original lease agreement is replaced by a new agreement with a new lessee, the lessor shall account for the termination of the original lease as provided in paragraph 842-30-40-2 and shall classify and account for the new lease as a separate transaction.
